Ethical Issues in 3D Printing - NCBI Author: National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI)
Abstract: This article discusses the ethical concerns of 3D printing, particularly in the realms of intellectual property, bioprinting, and sustainability. It examines how the ability to print objects on demand challenges traditional patent laws and raises concerns about counterfeiting. Additionally, the article explores the moral dilemmas of bioprinting, including the implications of printing human organs and tissues. Lastly, it addresses the environmental impact of 3D printing materials and the need for sustainable solutions.
Legal and Ethical Considerations in 3D Printing - Ian Coll McEachern Author: Ian Coll McEachern
Abstract: This article examines the legal ramifications of 3D printing, focusing on copyright infringement, counterfeiting, and the challenges of regulating this rapidly advancing technology. It discusses the increasing availability of 3D-printed firearms and the legal loopholes that exist in controlling their production. The article also highlights the difficulties in enforcing intellectual property laws in a world where digital blueprints can be easily shared and modified.
